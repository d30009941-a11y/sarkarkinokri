PO vs CLERK: AT A GLANCE

Feature SBI PO (Probationary Officer) SBI Clerk (Junior Associate)
Qualification Graduation in any discipline Graduation in any discipline
Age Limit 21 to 30 Years 20 to 28 Years
Selection Rounds Prelims + Mains + Psychometric + Interview Prelims + Mains (No Interview)
Bond Period Typical 2-Year Service Bond No major service bond
Final Merit Mains (Phase II) + Interview (Phase III) Mains Marks Only

THE 4-STAGE SELECTION JOURNEY

1
Preliminary Examination: 100 Marks (1 Hour). Objective test to filter candidates. Score is NOT added to final merit.
2
Main Examination: High-difficulty objective + Descriptive test. This is the "Elimination" round.
3
LPT (Local Language Test): Mandatory for Clerks to ensure they can communicate with regional customers.
4
Group Exercise & Interview: (PO Only). Tests leadership, temperament, and banking awareness.

Examiner's Note to Aspirants

State Bank of India recruitment is distinct from IBPS. SBI sets its own difficulty standards, often introducing new question types first. While IBPS follows a predictable pattern, SBI focuses on concept-heavy reasoning and data-intensive Quant. We recommend starting your preparation with our 90-Day Blueprint below.
