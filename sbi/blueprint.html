SBI 2026: The "Elite 90" Strategic Blueprint

Note for Aspirants: This 4-Phase plan is designed for SBI PO (Probationary Officer) but covers 100% of the SBI Clerk & SO (General Aptitude) syllabus. Prepare for the toughest to clear the rest.
Weeks 1-3

🏗️ Phase 1: The Concept Foundation

Eliminate calculation fear. If you can't solve a quadratic equation in 20 seconds, you aren't ready yet.

  • Quant: Learn Tables up to 30, Squares up to 50, and Cubes up to 20.
  • Reasoning: Master Alpha-Numeric Series and "Chinese" Coding-Decoding.
  • English: Focus on Grammar rules (120 Rules of Grammar) + Daily 'The Hindu' Editorial.
  • GA: Focus on "Banking Awareness" static terms (CRR, SLR, Repo Rate).
Weeks 4-7

🧱 Phase 2: Structural Speed & Arithmetic

This phase is where most candidates fail. Focus on the "language" of word problems.

  • Arithmetic: Mastery of Percentage, Ratio, and Average (The base of DI).
  • DI: Practice 5 sets of Mixed Graphs (Pie + Table) daily.
  • Reasoning: Linear Seating Arrangement (Single/Double Row) & Basic Floor Puzzles.
  • Resource Tip: Use sectional mocks to find your "weak-link" subject.
Weeks 8-10

🚀 Phase 3: The Mains & Descriptive Hybrid

Don't wait for Prelims results to start Mains prep. They only give you 15 days later.

  • Mains Reasoning: Input-Output, Coded Inequalities, and High-level Puzzles.
  • Data Analysis: Solve Logical DI and Missing DI sets.
  • Descriptive: Type one Letter and one Essay on a notepad/computer every 3 days.
  • GA: Revise the last 6 months of Current Affairs (Financial focus).
Weeks 11-12

⚖️ Phase 4: The Stress Test (Exam Simulation)

Purely mock-driven. Analyze your "Left-over" questions more than the "Solved" ones.

  • Daily Mock: 1 Prelims Mock at the exact time of your actual exam shift.
  • Analysis: Spend 2 hours analyzing 1 hour of mock test.
  • Buffer Days: Keep the last 3 days for light revision and mindset coaching.
