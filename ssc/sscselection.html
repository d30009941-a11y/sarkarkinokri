SSC Selection Posts: Official Guide to Multi-Level Recruitment

1. THE UNIQUE SELECTION STRUCTURE

Unlike other SSC exams, "Selection Posts" represent hundreds of different specialized job roles across various Government Departments, categorized into three distinct educational levels.

Exam LevelEligibilityExam TypeCommon Posts
Matriculation 10th Pass Objective (CBT) MTS, Driver, Lab Attendant, Library Clerk.
Higher Secondary 12th Pass Objective (CBT) Data Entry Operator, LDC, Store Keeper.
Graduation & Above Bachelor's Degree Objective (CBT) Section Officer, Senior Investigator, Junior Engineer.

2. COMPUTER BASED EXAMINATION (CBE) PATTERN

There is a single objective-type exam for each level. If you apply for multiple posts at the same level, you only need to appear for one exam for that level.

3. POST-SPECIFIC SYLLABUS HIGHLIGHTS

Syllabus LevelSubject Focus Area
Matric Level Focus on basic calculation, grammar, and general environment knowledge. Questions are designed to test a 10th-grade standard.
Intermediate Level Algebra, Geometry, and Trigonometry are added to Maths. Reasoning involves more complex analytical patterns.
Graduation Level Advanced levels of English (Comprehension/Cloze Test) and Mathematics (Profit & Loss, Time & Distance) comparable to SSC CGL standards.

4. SCRUTINY & FINAL SELECTION

  • Shortlisting: Based on CBE marks, candidates are called for Document Verification/Scrutiny in a ratio of 1:30 or 1:15 depending on vacancies.
  • Skill Test: Certain posts require Typing, Data Entry, or Computer Proficiency Tests which are qualifying in nature.
  • Regional Selection: Candidates must send hard copies of their applications and documents to the respective Regional Office (NR, WR, CR, etc.) after clearing the written exam.
