RRB ALP 2026: The Comprehensive Technical & Psychological Protocol

1. Eligibility: More Than Just Degrees

Most portals tell you that an ITI or Diploma is enough. **They are wrong.** ALP is a high-responsibility safety post. Your technical degree is only the entry ticket; your medical health is the boarding pass.

Educational Nuance: While B.E./B.Tech graduates are eligible, the exam pattern favors those with strong Basic Science & Engineering (BS&E) skills. If you are an Engineer, do not underestimate the ITI-level Trade Paper (Part B); it has a 35% qualifying bar that kills thousands of "over-qualified" dreams every year.

⚠️ The "A-1" Medical Death-Trap

Railway Medical Standards are the strictest in India. For ALP, the A-1 Standard is mandatory:

  • Lasik/Surgery: Even if your vision is now 6/6, if a doctor finds traces of Lasik, you are Permanently Debarred.
  • Color Blindness: Tested via Ishihara Plates. If you can't see the numbers in the dots, you cannot be a Pilot.
  • Night Vision: You will be tested in total darkness to ensure you can distinguish Signal Colors (Red/Yellow/Green) at high speeds.

2. The Selection Architecture & Merit Logic

Success in ALP is about understanding the **Merit Weightage**. You are not fighting for 100 marks; you are fighting for a weighted score.

Final Merit Formula

(70% of CBT-2 Part A) + (30% of CBAT Score) = Your Rank

*CBT-1 and CBT-2 Part B are Qualifying only.

Stage 1: CBT-1 (The Filter) - 75 Qns / 60 Mins. Focus 100% on Math and Science. Reasoning is usually easy here.
Stage 2: CBT-2 (The Decider) - Split into two parts.
• Part A (100 Qns): This is your life. 40 Marks are dedicated to "Basic Science & Engineering."
• Part B (75 Qns): Your chosen trade. 35% (26.25 marks) is required. If you get 26.24, you are out, even if you topped Part A.
Stage 3: CBAT (The Psycho Test) - 5 Batteries (Memory, Depth Perception, Concentration, etc.). You must score the minimum "T-Score" in each battery separately.

3. Expert Syllabus Breakdown: The "Surgical" Topics

Technical Pillar (Part A)

BS&E: This isn't normal physics. Focus on: Levers & Simple Machines, Velocity & Acceleration, Work Power Energy, and Heat & Temperature. These carry 40% weightage.

Trade Pillar (Part B)

Electrician/Fitter/Mechanical: Focus on Safety & Tools, Hand Tools, and Basic Workshop Calculations. Don't go into advanced engineering; stay at the ITI-Syllabus level.

4. The Documentation & Application Protocol

Small errors in the application lead to "Rejected" status months later. Follow this mentor-checked list:

Photo Specifications: White background, no caps, no glasses. The photo must be identical to the one you carry to the exam center.
Zone Strategy: Research the RRB Zone vacancies. Some zones like Secunderabad or Mumbai have high vacancies but higher cutoffs. Malda or Siliguri might have lower cutoffs but fewer seats. Choose wisely; you cannot change your RRB once submitted.

Documents Required for DV (Keep Ready)

  • 10th/Matriculation Certificate (For Date of Birth proof).
  • ITI/Diploma/Degree Semester-wise Marksheets.
  • Caste Certificate (OBC-NCL must be issued within 1 year).
  • Income Certificate (For EBC candidates claiming fee concession).
  • Original Aadhaar Card (For Biometric Verification at exam center).
