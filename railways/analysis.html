1. Mathematics (25-30 Marks)

TCS has shifted from "Formula-Based" to "Calculation-Heavy" questions. 80% of the paper is Arithmetic-driven.

Number System & BODMAS5-7 Questions
Time, Work & Pipes3-4 Questions
SI, CI & Profit/Loss4 Questions
Mensuration (2D/3D Formulas)2 Questions
Elementary Statistics (Mean/Median/Mode)2 Questions
🔥 The Calculation Trap:

In recent shifts, TCS places 2 questions with 4-digit decimals (e.g., 0.0045 * 1.23). Mentor Insight: These are "Time-Killers." Skip them and return only after finishing the Reasoning section.

2. General Science (25 Marks)

This is where selections are made. Railway Science is significantly tougher than SSC Science. It is 100% Concept-based.

Focus on Groups 1, 2, 17, and 18 for Chemistry weightage.

Subject-Wise Focus:

  • Physics (10-12 Qs): Focus on Light (Numerical), Electricity (Ohm's Law), and Magnetism. Formulas like $P = \frac{V^2}{R}$ or $\frac{1}{f} = \frac{1}{v} + \frac{1}{u}$ are mandatory.
  • Biology (8-10 Qs): Focus on Human Systems (Heart, Brain, Digestion) and Plant Kingdom. 90% of questions come from NCERT Life Processes.
  • Chemistry (4-5 Qs): Focus on Periodic Table and Chemical Reactions. You must know common names (e.g., Bleaching Powder = $CaOCl_2$).

3. General Intelligence (30 Marks)

Reasoning is the "Scoring Subject." A serious aspirant must aim for 28/30 here.

Syllogism (Statement-Conclusion)4-5 Questions
Seating Arrangement (Linear/Circular)3 Questions
Coding-Decoding (Number-Symbol)4 Questions
Mathematical Operations3 Questions
Expert Strategy: Solve Syllogism using the Venn Diagram method. It reduces error probability to zero. For Seating Arrangements, don't panic—TCS arrangements are usually 1-step logic.

4. General Awareness (20 Marks)

Aspirants waste 70% of their time here for only 20 marks. Be Smart.

  • Current Affairs (8-10 Qs): Only last 12 months. Focus on Sports, Awards, and Railway Budgets.
  • History/Polity (4-5 Qs): Modern History (1857-1947) and Articles of the Constitution.
  • Railway GK (2 Qs): First train, Zones, Headquarters, and New Projects (Kavach, Vande Bharat).
